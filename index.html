\(L^p\) Theory

\(L^p\) spaces are among the most famous examples of function spaces, typically taken to be \(L^{p}(\Omega, \mathcal{M}, \mu_{L})\) where \((\mathcal{M}, \mu_{L})\) is the Lebesgue measurable functions alongside the Lebesgue measure.
